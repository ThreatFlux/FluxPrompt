# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We want FluxPrompt to be a professional, respectful, and technically rigorous project. Contributors, maintainers, and community members are expected to make participation harassment-free for everyone.

## Expected Behavior

- Be respectful and constructive in reviews, issues, and discussions.
- Assume good intent, but challenge technical decisions with evidence.
- Keep feedback specific, actionable, and focused on the work.
- Accept that maintainers may prioritize security, correctness, and project scope over preference.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, personal attacks, or discriminatory language
- Trolling, intimidation, or sustained disruption
- Publishing private information without permission
- Abusive conduct in issues, pull requests, discussions, chat, or other project spaces

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within project spaces and in public spaces when someone is representing FluxPrompt or the ThreatFlux project community.

## Enforcement

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing these standards. They may remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, documentation, issues, or other contributions that violate this Code of Conduct.

## Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, report it privately to the project maintainers through GitHub's private maintainer communication channels. Reports will be reviewed promptly and handled as confidentially as practical.
